The Brotherhood

Pi Kappa Alpha · Minnesota

Before the tee times, there was the house.

The shield at the top of this page is borrowed with pride — it's molded from the emblem of Pi Kappa Alpha. Every man in this field was a brother of the fraternity's University of Minnesota chapter, Class of ’22, and the tournament takes its name from the chapter house where it all started: 1813.

Graduation scattered the group across the country, but the group chat never went quiet. Four years later they remain, officially and to this date, the bestest of friends — and the 1813 Classic exists to keep it that way. Once a year, twenty brothers, one course, no excuses.

The Standard

Pace of Play & Betting

Surrendering the hole

These are nice courses and pace matters. Higher handicappers — you won't finish every hole, and that's fine. If you're taking 8–10 strokes and the other team has clearly won the hole, pick up and move on.

Betting

The team point structure covers the weekend title only — specifics on team betting to be discussed soon. Within your individual matches, you're encouraged to bet skins or whatever else you like.
